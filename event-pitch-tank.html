What is Idea Pitching?

The Idea Pitching Event is a platform designed to encourage students to present innovative ideas, startup concepts, and problem-solving solutions in a structured and competitive environment. The event aims to promote entrepreneurship, creativity, and critical thinking among students across all departments.

Participants will pitch their ideas individually or in teams before a panel of judges within a fixed time limit, followed by a brief Q&A session. The focus will be on originality to promote problem-solving skills, feasibility, scalability, and real-world impact of the ideas.

Procedure

01

Problem Analysis

Pre-requisite

Understand and define the scope of the problem to develop an impactful solution.

02

Propose a Solution

Before Event

Develop a solution and present it through a pitch deck.

03

Pitching and Presentation

Final Round

Deliver a 6-minute pitch followed by 2 minutes of Q&A with the jury.

Rules & Regulations

Team Structure

Individual or Team participation (Max 4 members). Each team can present only one idea.

Time Limit

6 Minutes Pitching + 2 Minutes Q&A. Strict adherence to time is required.

Content

Idea must be original. Plagiarism leads to disqualification. Prototype not mandatory (Concepts allowed).

Monitoring

Presentation slides must be submitted before deadline. Jury decision is final/binding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can beginners participate?

Yes! This event is for all skill levels. We provide mentors to help beginners.

Is there a registration fee?

No, it's completely free.

Can I participate solo?

Solo participants can join existing teams or form new teams at the event.

What's the judging criteria?

Innovation, Technical execution, Impact, Presentation
